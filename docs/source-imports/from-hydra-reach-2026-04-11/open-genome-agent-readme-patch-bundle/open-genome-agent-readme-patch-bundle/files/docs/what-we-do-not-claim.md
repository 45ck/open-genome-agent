# What We Do Not Claim

This repo should be explicit about its limits.

## We do not claim diagnostic certainty

- We do not claim that this repo can diagnose disease.
- We do not claim that a strong-looking variant finding is the same thing as a clinical diagnosis.
- We do not claim that benchmark precision and recall equal clinical validity.

## We do not claim that weak signals are strong signals

- We do not claim that PRS or trait-style outputs belong in the same confidence bucket as direct variant findings.
- We do not claim that exploratory or probabilistic outputs should be treated like hard answers.
- We do not claim that a polished report removes the need for caveats.

## We do not claim that public data removes responsibility

- We do not claim that a public profile or public genome file removes the need for limitations and uncertainty.
- We do not claim that public-demo convenience changes the interpretation bar.

## We do not claim that one source settles everything

- We do not claim that functional assay evidence alone settles final clinical significance.
- We do not claim that one database, one benchmark, or one annotation pass is enough on its own.

## We do not claim that pharmacogenomics output is self-executing

- We do not claim that pharmacogenomics guidance should be followed without human review.
- We do not claim that this repo can tell someone to start, stop, or change medication.
